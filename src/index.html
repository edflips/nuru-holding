Our Vision

To provide 5 million delighted clients with rock-solid renewable energy by September 30, 2024.

About Us

Nuru is a Congolese company focused on connectivity throughout eastern Congo. We built our first solar-based energy site in Beni, and are now constructing a 1.3MW hybrid site in Goma, which will be one of the largest off-grid solar projects in all of sub-Saharan Africa.

Nuru was first established under the name Kivu Green Energy in August 2015, when we acquired the largest commercial energy provider in Beni, North Kivu. The following year, we oversaw a 35kW solar installation at a university in Beni, and in June 2017 installed our first commercial solar PV and storage system. It was the first of its kind in eastern Congo and provides affordable and reliable electricity to over 60 households, small- or medium-sized enterprises, and NGOs. It is a 55kW solar-based mini-grid in the Boikene neighborhood of Beni with a 100kVa back-up genset.

The Nuru Team

Jonathan E. Shaw

|Chief Executive Officer and Co-Founder

Jonathan is the co-founder and CEO of Nuru. Jon completed his PhD in African History at the University of Michigan in 2017, and is a business and social impact entrepreneur several times over. He is the founder of Café Kivu, a business aimed at creating a culture of coffee roasting and consumption in eastern DRC. Prior to that, from 2013 to 2015, he founded and directed the Integrated Research Institute (IRI) at the Université Chrétienne Bilingue du Congo in Beni, DRC. Thanks to his catalytic leadership, IRI is now a leading research institute on land governance and agribusiness research (e.g., coffee and cocoa) in DRC. Jon and his family have been actively engaged in DRC since 2013. Jon is based in Nairobi, Kenya.

Juliana Philippa Kerrest

Juliana joined Nuru in February 2019. She has an MBA from the MIT Sloan School of Management, with a focus on sustainability, and an MPA from the Harvard Kennedy School, where she focused on the crossroads of business and social impact. She was a recipient of the 2017 MIT Sloan Social Impact Fellowship and the 2016 MIT Sloan Sustainable Business MBA Internship Award. Her background is in entrepreneurship and technology, with experience in both the public and private sectors. Juliana has worked at a variety of organizations, from a nonprofit focused on female entrepreneurship, to her own tech startup, to Amazon.com. She has extensive international experience and has previously worked in eastern Congo. Juliana is based in Goma, DRC.

Baraka Kasali

Baraka joined Nuru in July 2018 as Chief Operating Officer, based in Goma. Now heading up the U.S. Nuru office, he is Director of Strategic Development and Partnerships. He has extensive executive leadership experience in the development sector, possesses a wealth of operational expertise, and has in-depth knowledge and familiarity with working in the DRC and within its complex political environment. Baraka is based in Illinois, USA.
